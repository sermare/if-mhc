Recovering and Redirecting Peptide Backbone Conformation via Contact-Conditioned Ca Generation in a Cross-Reactive pMHC/TCR System

Authors

Some T-cell receptors are cross-reactive: they recognize several different peptides by letting each peptide fold into a different shape inside the same MHC groove. This cross-reactivity is a central safety problem for TCR-based therapies, where an engineered receptor can cause dangerous off-target effects by recognizing an unintended self-peptide that adopts a compatible backbone shape. Deep generative models can now build protein backbones with high accuracy, which raises a question that bears directly on that problem: given only the receptor side of the interaction, can such a model be steered to build a peptide backbone in one chosen target shape, even a shape that is not the one closest to its input? We test this in a well-studied two-peptide system, the DMF5 receptor on HLA-A*02:01, which binds two 10-residue peptides (GIG and DRG) whose backbones differ by 2.87 A. Conditioning only on receptor-side contacts, we generated 300 designs per crystal for 20 different conditioning schemes and scored them against a three-criterion register test calibrated to native molecular-dynamics ensembles at physiological 310 K, correcting for a backbone-threading artifact that raw RMSD cannot detect. Recovery and redirection are achievable but rare (4 and 3 designs of 8,722; best 1.07 and 1.44 A). Critically, our no-conditioning control produced zero designs that dock or approach either backbone in RMSD (0 of 932), marking a step toward anticipating the cross-reactive, off-target peptide recognition that constrains TCR therapy safety.
