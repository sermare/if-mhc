Recovering and Redirecting Peptide Backbone Conformation via Contact-Conditioned Ca Generation in a Cross-Reactive pMHC/TCR System

Authors

Cross-reactive TCR/pMHC systems, in which one receptor engages chemically distinct peptides through distinct bound backbone conformations, raise a question orthogonal to conventional epitope design: can a structure-conditioned generative model be steered, from receptor-side information alone, toward a specific target backbone conformation, including, deliberately, one other than the conformation nearest its conditioning input? Using a minimal two-state system, the DMF5 TCR on HLA-A*02:01, which accommodates two decameric peptides (GIG and DRG) via backbones separated by 2.87 A Ca RMSD, we ask whether RFdiffusion, conditioned only on receptor-side contact residues and given no peptide-backbone template, can generate a Ca backbone that recovers a peptide's own native conformation or crosses into the alternate one. We generated a converged corpus of 24,831 designs across 20 conditioning schemes and scored them against a three-criterion register test calibrated to native molecular-dynamics ensembles at physiological 310 K rather than to a single crystal, correcting for a backbone-threading artifact that raw RMSD cannot detect. Both recovery and redirection are achievable but rare (7 and 4 de novo designs of 16,919; best 1.07 and 1.44 A), gated by the breadth of conditioning rather than any curated residue set. Critically, our no-conditioning control produced zero designs that dock or approach either native backbone in RMSD (0 of 1,533), establishing these events as real signal above a zero-information background. A templating control recovers register almost at will, locating it in a spatially confined C-terminal signal that receptor-side contacts only weakly determine.
