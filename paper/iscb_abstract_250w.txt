Recovering and Redirecting Peptide Backbone Conformation via Contact-Conditioned Ca Generation in a Cross-Reactive pMHC/TCR System

Authors

Some T-cell receptors recognize several different peptides by letting each peptide fold into a different shape inside the same MHC groove. Deep generative models can now build protein backbones and design their sequences with high accuracy, which raises a practical question: given only the receptor side of the interaction, can such a model be steered to build a peptide backbone in one chosen target shape, even a shape that is not the one closest to its input? We test this in a well-studied two-peptide system, the DMF5 receptor on HLA-A*02:01, which binds two 10-residue peptides (GIG and DRG) whose backbones differ by 2.87 A Ca RMSD. Conditioning only on receptor-side contact residues, with no peptide-backbone template, we generated 300 designs per crystal for each of 20 conditioning schemes and scored them against a three-criterion register test calibrated to native molecular-dynamics ensembles at physiological 310 K rather than to a single crystal, correcting for a backbone-threading artifact that raw RMSD cannot detect. Both recovery and redirection are achievable but rare (4 and 3 de novo designs of 8,722; best 1.07 and 1.44 A), gated by the breadth of conditioning rather than any curated residue set. Critically, our no-conditioning control produced zero designs that dock or approach either native backbone in RMSD (0 of 932), establishing these events as real signal above a zero-information background. A templating control recovers register on demand, locating it in a spatially confined tail-end signal that receptor-side contacts only weakly determine.
